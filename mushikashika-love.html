The Story

"An online romance. A loan shark's shadow. And a city that never lets you leave clean."

Logline

Nandi thought she'd fallen in love with Thabo — a charming, attentive man she met through her phone screen. For six months, he sent voice notes at midnight, remembered her favourite playlist, and promised to fly her to Zimbabwe for New Year's. She came with a suitcase full of hope. What she found was Harare.

Tafa drives a mushikashika — an unlicensed taxi threading through Harare's backstreets like a man who knows exactly where every secret is buried. He's funny, sharp, and deeply in debt to Micho: a loan shark who owns half the informal economy and all of Tafa's future. When Tafa is sent to intercept Nandi at the airport, what begins as an errand becomes the most complicated fare he's ever taken.

Part romance, part chase, all Harare — Mushikashika Love is a film about the difference between the life you perform online and the one you have to live in the streets. Set against the city's golden-hour glow and electric night markets, it asks: when danger is this beautiful, how far will you go for real love?

N

The Visitor

Nandi

Johannesburg, South Africa

A sharp-tongued logistics manager who trusted her heart over her instincts. She came to Harare chasing love and won't leave until she finds the truth.

T

The Driver

Tafa

Mbare, Harare

Knows every pothole in Harare's streets and every loophole in its unspoken rules. Loyal to a fault — which is exactly why Micho owns him.

M

The Loan Shark

Micho

Harare CBD

All warmth on the surface, all calculation underneath. He's never raised his voice in his life — he doesn't need to. Everyone already knows what's at stake.

C

The Ally

Chido

Harare North

Tafa's cousin, Nandi's unlikely confidante. Runs a street food stall that doubles as the neighbourhood news service. She knows everything — and shares it for the right price.

The World

A city that lives
between dusk and debt.

Harare is not a backdrop. It is a character. Every alley, every deal, every hesitation belongs to this city.

Harare Streets

From the golden avenues of Borrowdale to the kinetic hum of Mbare Musika, Harare moves at its own rhythm — beautiful, layered, and impossible to fake.

The Hustle Economy

Mushikashika taxis, rooftop vendors, informal lenders — Harare's parallel economy runs on trust, nerve, and the unspoken knowledge of who you owe.

Love Under Pressure

When circumstances are this volatile, love stops being a luxury and becomes an act of defiance. Nandi and Tafa have no business falling for each other — which is exactly why they do.
